First Amendment, 508(c)(1)(A) & International Protections

Masjid Al-Tawheed stands under the protections of the First Amendment to the U.S. Constitution: “Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof.”

Masjid Al-Tawheed also operates under the inherent protections of 508(c)(1)(A), which affirms that churches, ministries, and their integrated auxiliaries are automatically tax-exempt and are not required to apply for recognition with the IRS.

Beyond national recognition, our ministry is affirmed by international law:

  • The Hague Convention — honoring human dignity, family jurisdiction, and non-commercial private assemblies.
  • The United Nations Charter (Articles 18–20) — upholding freedom of belief, peaceful association, and religious expression.
  • The Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties — validating private ecclesiastical contracts and trust instruments under spiritual law.

Together, these protections ensure our ministry’s works, trusts, and assemblies remain free, spiritual, and sovereign.
